## Primary sources

1. Webern, Anton. *Fünf Stücke für Orchester, op. 10*. Vienna: Universal
   Edition, UE 5067/12416, 1923. The score supplies instrumentation, registers,
   durations, dynamics, articulations, techniques, tempo words, and the distinct
   physiognomy of the five movements.
2. Webern, Anton. *Der Weg zur neuen Musik*. Edited by Willi Reich. Vienna:
   Universal Edition, 1960. The lectures supply Webern's concepts of
   *Faßlichkeit*, *Zusammenhang*, *Gliederung*, variation, and presentation of
   musical thought.

## Analytical and biographical sources

1. Reutter, Hans Peter. “Anton Webern (1883-1945) - Fünf Stücke für Orchester
   op. 10 (1911-13) - Freie Atonalität, Reduktion auf aphoristische Gesten.”
   *Wege durch das frühe 20. Jahrhundert*. n.d. Reutter supports the B/H-F
   trajectory, displaced symmetries, three-note cells, metrical veiling, pitch
   counts, and instrumental correspondences.
2. Kholopova, Valentina, and Yuri Kholopov. *Anton Webern: Zhizn' i
   tvorchestvo*. Moscow: Sovetskii kompozitor, 1984. The monograph supplies
   biographical context, the continuity between early and late Webern,
   compressed form, musical time, and the role of timbre and silence.
3. Zeller, Matthew. “Klangfarbenmelodie in 1911: Timbre's Functional Roles in
   Webern's Opp. 9 and 10.” *Music Theory Online* 28, no. 1 (2022).
   https://doi.org/10.30535/mto.28.1.9. Zeller supports the codependence of
   timbre, pitch, register, articulation, and dynamics and cites Webern's
   correspondence concerning a “Wechsel der Farben.”
4. Ballance, Joshua. “Pitch-Class Distributions in the Music of Anton Webern.”
   In *Proceedings of CMMR 2019*, 2020. This corpus study provides a quantitative
   external check on the exceptional pitch-class distributions within op. 10.

## Archival orientation

The Anton Webern Gesamtausgabe documents sources, genesis, revisions, and
performance history: https://anton-webern.ch/. The Library of Congress guide
locates correspondence collections:
https://guides.loc.gov/anton-webern/correspondence. The Paul Sacher Foundation
describes the Anton Webern Collection and the edition of the letters to Heinrich
Jalowetz: https://www.paul-sacher-stiftung.ch/.

## Transfer rule

A score-observable fact may become a hard constraint. An analytical relation
may become a weighted tendency. A numerical probability without corpus counts
must remain labelled as a heuristic. This separation is retained in
`max/webern_persona_profiles.json`.
