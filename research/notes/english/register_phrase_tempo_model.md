## Register model

The ranges below are conservative sounding MIDI ranges. The ordinary band is
the statistical default; the outer band remains available for a structural
purpose.

| Instrument | Physical range | Ordinary band |
|---|---:|---:|
| Flute | C4-A6 | G4-D6 |
| Clarinet | D3-G6 | G3-B-flat5 |
| Trumpet | F-sharp3-B-flat5 | B-flat3-E5 |
| Trombone | E2-G4 | A2-D4 |
| Celesta | C3-C7 | G3-E6 |
| Harp | C2-C7 | G2-E6 |
| Glockenspiel | G4-G6 | C5-D6 |
| Violin | G3-C7 | C4-E6 |
| Viola | C3-B-flat5 | F3-D5 |
| Violoncello | C2-E5 | G2-G4 |

Register is evaluated in relation to dynamic level, activity, formal axis, and
the previous event in the same voice. Large leaps are possible, but repeated
occupation of an extreme register receives a strong penalty.

## Micro-phrase and temporary focus

A micro-phrase is a group of two to six events with one contour, rhythmic cell,
and instrumental function. The foreground instrument may retain two or more
notes, while background colours answer, extend, or resonate. Phrase slurs are
assigned only to coherent multi-note spans; detached notes in another voice may
occur simultaneously.

## Conditions for common rhythm

Shared rhythm is admitted when the score articulates a brief block, a family
response, a culmination, or the late clarification characteristic of movements
II and V. It is not a default texture. A homorhythmic event shares onset and
duration; the following group is required to select a different rhythmic cell.
Unrelated instruments do not inherit a previous phrase's sixteenth-note
pattern.

## Movement III pedal

The pedal field of movement III is modeled as a focal pitch class distributed
among a maximum of three carriers. Their attacks and durations are staggered,
and their articulation may differ between tremolo and trill. This produces a
resonant field without the visually and acoustically excessive duplication of
several identical sustained layers.

## Global tempo

Tempo changes are score-level objects. Movement profiles combine markings such
as *zögernd*, *drängend*, *poco rit.*, *molto rit.*, *Zeit lassen*, and *a
tempo* with numerical tempo ratios. A rare one-measure change to 3/4, 2/4, or
5/8 may articulate a local deflection before the return to 4/4.

## Source basis

The ranges are performance-oriented project constraints; the formal uses of
register, symmetry, pedal, and tempo derive from direct score inspection,
Reutter's analysis, Kholopova and Kholopov's account of Webern's musical time,
and Zeller's analysis of timbral function.
